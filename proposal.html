02 Target market

Four people who have their mornings free.

A 9am weekday slot is a gift and a filter at the same time. It rules out office workers, so we aim only at people whose mornings are their own. Four segments, in priority order.

PrimarySegment A

Active over 50s & retirees

The Southern Suburbs have a large, health aware older community with free mornings. Doctors routinely tell them to try water based movement, and they fear high impact exercise. They value friendliness, safety and routine, and they pay monthly without fuss when they feel looked after.

Where we reach them

Facebook feed (heavy users), local community groups, retirement estates, GP and physio waiting rooms, Google search for low impact exercise.

PrimarySegment B

Rehab & low impact seekers

People recovering from knee or hip surgery, back trouble, arthritis or a long injury layoff. They arrive with a need, not a want, so they convert brilliantly. Often sent by physios, biokineticists, GPs and orthopaedic surgeons, which makes referral the key channel here.

Where we reach them

Practitioner referral cards, biokineticist and physio practices within 5km, hydrotherapy searches, targeted rehab interest ads.

SecondarySegment C

New & expecting mums

Postnatal mums easing back into movement, and expecting mums (who route to Heather's 10am Prenatal class). School drop off lands right before 9am, so the timing is natural. They talk constantly in local groups, so one happy mum brings three more. A powerful word of mouth feeder.

Where we reach them

Instagram feed and stories, Southern Suburbs mommy groups, school and playgroup networks, the referral engine.

SecondarySegment D

Work from home locals

People who run their day from home nearby and want a real reason to leave the desk mid morning. A smaller group, but very cheap to reach because they are hyper local, and the 9am timing suits a flexible day perfectly.

Where we reach them

Tight radius Meta ads, neighbourhood community groups, Google Business Profile, organic Instagram.

We deliberately ignore office commuters, the hardcore fitness crowd, students and tourists. Chasing them wastes budget. Focus is the strategy.

04 Channel strategy

Six channels, all pointing at one WhatsApp.

Ranked by expected return for this specific 9am, hyper local offer. Paid does the reach, free does the trust, and WhatsApp does the conversion.

Channel 1, the workhorse

Meta ads (FB + Insta)

Role
Paid reach. Click to WhatsApp ads drop enquiries straight into Heather's phone. A tight 8km circle around the pool, layered by age and interest.
Rough reach
Tens of thousands of local impressions per month on a small budget.
What good looks like
Cost per enquiry under R80, and a steady flow of warm WhatsApp chats.
Channel 2, free trust

Community groups

Role
Free, high trust. One genuine, friendly post from Heather in each Southern Suburbs group, plus helpful replies, beats paid ads for trust.
Rough reach
Thousands of hyper local, highly relevant members per group.
What good looks like
Warm comments and DMs, no spammy tone, posted as a real person every week.
Channel 3, highest quality

Practitioner referral

Role
Physios, biokineticists, GPs and orthopaedic practices within 5km. A one page referral card plus a warm intro from Heather.
Rough reach
Low volume, but the highest intent leads of all.
What good looks like
Two or three practices sending patients, at almost zero cost. A standing weekly habit.
Channel 4, high intent

Google Business Profile

Role
Free and essential. Captures people actively searching aqua aerobics near me and water fitness Claremont.
Rough reach
Every high intent local searcher, plus map visibility.
What good looks like
A complete profile with photos, 9am times, the WhatsApp link and real reviews.
Channel 5, the engine

WhatsApp conversion

Role
Where every channel funnels. Enquiries become bookings, and bookings become regulars through friendly reminders.
Rough reach
100% of enquiries pass through here.
What good looks like
An instant auto reply, then a personal reply within the hour. Speed of reply is the highest leverage thing in the funnel.
Channel 6, keeps it alive

Organic social

Role
Two to three posts a week: calm water, a real class moment, a member win, a low impact tip.
Rough reach
Followers plus anyone an ad sends to check the page.
What good looks like
A page that looks alive and warm when a new person lands on it. Feeds the ads with content.

07 Four week launch calendar

From setup to full classes in a month.

A clear rhythm: prove the message cheaply, tighten what works, create real momentum, then lock in retention. Free channels run every single week alongside the ads.

Week 0
Setup, before spend
  • Confirm prices and class capacity
  • Collect 3 testimonials, photos, 1 short video
  • Connect Heather's own payment link
  • Publish landing page, test every button
  • Build the Meta campaign, leave it paused
  • Load WhatsApp auto replies
Week 1
Prove the message
  • Mon launch ads, post in 2 local groups, first organic post
  • Tue reply to every enquiry within the hour, referral cards to 2 practices
  • Wed check cost per enquiry, post 2
  • Thu bring a friend nudge in class, one more group post
  • Fri 5 minute KPI check, kill any dud creative
Week 2
Tighten & lift
  • Mon if the numbers are good, step up spend, refresh best creatives
  • Tue two more practitioner referrals
  • Wed test a short class video ad, mid week glance
  • Thu collect a fresh photo or testimonial
  • Fri KPI check, update the fill tracker
Week 3+4
Momentum & retention
  • W3 push the true "filling up, a few spots left" message
  • W3 feature a real member story, ask for Google reviews
  • W4 focus shifts to keeping new members, personal rebooking nudges
  • W4 full month review, set the steady state budget
  • W4 start a small waitlist for the fuller day

What Heather controls

Everything. The WhatsApp Business app (free) runs the greeting, away message, quick replies and labels. Her own payment link handles money. A simple sheet or phone note tracks who is booked. Every rand goes straight to Heather.

Class payments flow directly into a gateway Heather owns and controls (SnapScan, Zapper, Yoco or PayFast). Nothing is ever routed through us or DMG, and we never hold a customer's card details. This is a hard rule, stated plainly for peace of mind.
